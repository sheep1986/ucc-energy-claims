Information We Collect

We collect the following personal information:

  • Contact Details: Name, business name, email address, phone number, postal address
  • Business Information: Company registration number, industry type, energy usage details
  • Financial Information: Energy contract details, billing information, payment preferences
  • Communication Records: Emails, phone calls, meeting notes, support interactions
  • Legal Documents: Energy contracts, correspondence with suppliers, claim documentation

When you visit our website, we may collect:

  • Device Information: IP address, browser type, operating system
  • Usage Data: Pages visited, time spent, click patterns
  • Cookies: Essential, functional, and analytics cookies (see our cookie policy)
  • Form Data: Information submitted through contact forms and calculators

We collect information through:

  • Direct Contact: Forms, emails, phone calls, meetings
  • Documents You Provide: Energy contracts, bills, correspondence
  • Third Parties: Energy suppliers (with your consent), public records
  • Website Analytics: Google Analytics and similar tools
  • Legal Processes: Court filings, regulatory submissions

Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data based on the following legal grounds under GDPR:

Contract Performance

Processing your claims, providing legal services, managing your case

Consent

Marketing communications, non-essential cookies, optional services

Legal Obligation

Regulatory compliance, court orders, professional conduct rules

Legitimate Interest

Fraud prevention, business development, website analytics

How We Share Your Information

We never sell your personal data to third parties.

We only share your information when necessary for service delivery or legal compliance.

We may share information with:

  • Energy Suppliers: To process your claims and negotiate settlements
  • Legal Counsel: Barristers and specialist legal advisors (under legal professional privilege)
  • IT Services: Secure cloud hosting, email services, document management
  • Payment Processors: For secure payment handling and banking
  • Professional Services: Accountants, auditors, insurance providers

All service providers are bound by confidentiality agreements and data processing contracts.

We may disclose information when required by:

  • Courts and Tribunals: Legal proceedings, court orders, witness statements
  • Regulators: SRA, ICO, Ofgem, FCA reporting requirements
  • Law Enforcement: Police investigations, fraud prevention agencies
  • Government Bodies: HMRC, insolvency practitioners, statutory audits

Your data is primarily processed within the UK/EEA. Limited international transfers may occur for:

  • Cloud Services: AWS, Microsoft Azure (with adequacy decisions or safeguards)
  • Customer Support: Secure third-party platforms with appropriate protections
  • Analytics: Google Analytics (with data anonymization)

All international transfers comply with GDPR transfer requirements including adequacy decisions, standard contractual clauses, or other approved mechanisms.

Your Privacy Rights

Right to Access

Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you, including details of processing activities.

Right to Rectification

Correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you.

Right to Erasure

Request deletion of your personal data (subject to legal and regulatory retention requirements).

Right to Restrict

Limit how we process your data while we investigate concerns or disputes.

Right to Portability

Receive your personal data in a structured, machine-readable format for transfer to another provider.

Right to Object

Object to processing based on legitimate interests, including direct marketing.

To exercise your rights, contact us at privacy@utilitycommissionclaims.co.uk or 0800 123 4567

We will respond within one month. Complex requests may take up to three months with notification.

Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data:

  • Encryption: Data encrypted in transit and at rest
  • Access Controls: Multi-factor authentication, role-based access
  • Regular Testing: Penetration testing, vulnerability assessments
  • Staff Training: Regular data protection and security awareness training
  • Incident Response: 24/7 monitoring and response procedures

Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary:

  • Active Cases: Duration of claim plus 6 years
  • Client Files: 6 years from case closure (SRA requirement)
  • Financial Records: 6 years from transaction date
  • Marketing Data: Until consent withdrawn or 3 years of inactivity
  • Website Analytics: 26 months (Google Analytics default)
