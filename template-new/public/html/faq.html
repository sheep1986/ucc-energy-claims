General Questions

Energy broker commissions are fees paid by energy suppliers to brokers for bringing them business. These commissions are often hidden within your energy rates rather than being disclosed separately. Under current regulations, you have the right to know about and recover these undisclosed commissions.

You may be eligible if you've used an energy broker to arrange your business energy contracts in the past 6 years. This includes situations where:

  • A broker arranged your energy contract but didn't fully disclose their commission
  • You weren't told the exact amount of commission being paid
  • Commission wasn't itemized separately on your bills
  • You feel the commission wasn't clearly explained

Recovery amounts vary significantly based on your energy usage and the commission rates applied. Our clients have recovered anywhere from £5,000 to over £100,000. Typical recoveries by industry:

  • Manufacturing: Average £45,000
  • Healthcare: Average £38,000
  • Education: Average £42,000
  • Hospitality: Average £32,000
  • Retail: Average £25,000

Use our free calculator to get an estimate for your business.

Many businesses don't realize they used a broker because they often present themselves as "energy consultants" or similar titles. Signs you may have used a broker include:

  • Someone contacted you offering better energy rates
  • You switched suppliers through a third party
  • Your contract was arranged by anyone other than the supplier directly
  • You received "free" energy procurement services

We can analyze your contracts to determine if brokers were involved.

Yes, there's typically a 6-year limitation period for contractual claims in England and Wales. However, the exact time limit can depend on when you discovered the undisclosed commission. We recommend starting your claim as soon as possible to ensure you don't miss out on recovery opportunities.

Legal & Compliance

Absolutely yes. Under UK consumer and commercial law, you have the right to:

  • Know about any commissions paid by suppliers to brokers
  • Have these commissions properly disclosed
  • Seek recovery if commissions were undisclosed or misrepresented
  • Fair treatment in commercial energy transactions

Ofgem regulations and recent court cases have strengthened consumers' rights in this area.

In our experience, making a legitimate commission claim does not negatively impact your ongoing relationship with your energy supplier. Here's why:

  • Claims are handled professionally through proper legal channels
  • Suppliers understand these are legitimate regulatory matters
  • Your current energy supply continues uninterrupted
  • Many suppliers now have dedicated teams for handling such claims

We maintain professional relationships with all major UK energy suppliers.

Several key regulations and legal principles support broker commission claims:

  • Ofgem Standards of Conduct: Require fair treatment and transparency
  • Consumer Rights Act 2015: Protects against unfair practices
  • Unfair Terms in Consumer Contracts Regulations: Covers disclosure requirements
  • Common Law Fiduciary Duties: Require brokers to act in clients' best interests
  • Contract Law: Right to know material terms affecting pricing
