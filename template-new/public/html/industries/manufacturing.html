Why Manufacturing is Targeted

Manufacturing facilities are attractive targets for undisclosed broker commissions due to their unique energy characteristics.

High Energy Usage

Manufacturing facilities consume significant amounts of electricity and gas, making even small commission percentages substantial.

Complex Pricing

Industrial energy contracts are complex, making it easy for brokers to hide commission payments within the rate structure.

Long Contracts

Multi-year contracts mean hidden commissions compound over time, creating larger recovery opportunities.

Common Hidden Charges in Manufacturing

Our analysis of manufacturing energy contracts reveals these common areas where undisclosed commissions are hidden.

Inflated Unit Rates

Base electricity and gas rates inflated above market price to include hidden broker commission.

Standing Charge Premiums

Daily standing charges increased beyond standard rates to cover commission payments.

Capacity Charges

High-usage manufacturers charged inflated capacity fees that include undisclosed broker payments.

Peak Rate Premiums

Manufacturing peak-time rates inflated to include commission on high-usage periods.
